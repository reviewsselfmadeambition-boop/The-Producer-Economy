1964 The Prophecy

McLuhan Told Us. No One Was Ready to Hear It.

Marshall McLuhan, a media theorist most people have never read, published a framework that perfectly describes why most creators fail to monetize: the medium shapes behavior before content is even created.

He argued that every new communication medium restructures the social and economic relationships of those who use it. The printing press didn't just deliver books — it created publishers and readers. Radio didn't just broadcast — it created broadcasters and audiences.

Social platforms didn't just create content — they created producers and consumers. The question was always: which one are you?

Now The Reality

The Digital Economy Has Two Classes. Most People Choose Wrong.

Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, X — these are not just platforms. They are economies with built-in class structures. At the top: producers who supply value and extract revenue. At the base: consumers who supply attention and generate revenue for producers.

The tragedy is that most people trying to "make it" on social media are operating with a consumer mindset inside a producer system. They are waiting to be discovered. They are creating reactively. They are building on borrowed land with a tenant's psychology.

The platform cannot pay you if your identity inside it is a consumer. It is not personal. It is structural.
